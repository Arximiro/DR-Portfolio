My Story

For a little over a year now I've been learning Web Development. It started off as a thing on the side, 10-15hrs per week on my days off. I wanted to see if it was something I enjoyed, and as it turns out I really did. In July 2017 I decided to commit to it full time and left my job. I had enough savings to get by for awhile if I lived cheap, and I wanted to give this my all. I've since spent 30-40hrs per week every week learning new things and improving existing skills.

I'm proficient writing HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I've also spent extensive time working with React. My first language was C# which really hammered the fundamentals into me. It was a difficult first language, but it was well worth the struggle because of the OOP concepts I learned. I'm most comfortable with Front End Development but I also know a fair amount of Back End.

Goals

I made the decision in early 2017 that I wanted to change my career and my future. I knew I was capable of more and a good friend got me started with C# and I discovered I really enjoy programming. Ever since then I've continued to learn and grow and in July of 2017 I took the big step of leaving my job to learn full time to accelerate my progress.

I've spent a little over a year learning what I need to secure a position as a Jr. Developer. That is my goal right now. I've done more courses than I can remember and continue to do more every day. At this point I believe I'm ready to begin my career, and I have been told by my friend that works as a Sr. Developer that I am more than ready to begin working.

Many have told me you learn at an incredibly accelerated pace when on the job. That's what I really want right now. A chance to get on the job and prove myself while continuing to learn and grow.

My big thing in life it to just be happy. Life is too short to do something you don't enjoy. That's why I made the decision to change my career while I was still young enough to do so.
